A small idea that could make digital health platforms much more powerful

Most digital health tools help clinicians communicate, document, triage, or coordinate.

Very few help people understand what to do next.

That gap matters more than it looks. Because confusion is one of the biggest hidden drivers of demand.

The missing layer

Support Playlists are personalised guidance paths generated from what someone says they need.

Support Playlists start with voice, and fall back to text.

Not search results.
Not static signposting.
Not generic advice.

They translate a person’s situation into a tailored set of next steps, drawing from local, national and digital support options.

In simple terms:
They help people reach the right help sooner, and with more confidence.

Why this matters for platforms

Most platforms currently optimise for interaction.

Messaging. Forms. Notes. Tasks. Triage.

Those are essential.

But there’s another layer that sits just outside them:

Navigation.

If people don’t know where they’re going, even the best systems still receive misdirected demand.

Where Support Playlists fit

They don’t replace platforms.
They strengthen them.

They can sit alongside existing tools and act as a guidance layer that:

• reduces unnecessary contacts
• improves pathway alignment
• supports prevention
• increases confidence
• reduces explanation time
• improves experience on both sides

Platforms help clinicians do things.
Support Playlists help people understand things.

Why now

Health systems everywhere are shifting:

From hospital to community
From analogue to digital
From treatment to prevention

Those shifts only work if people can navigate support before they escalate into services.

Navigation is becoming infrastructure.

The bigger pattern
