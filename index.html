Where this helps a COO

1. Fewer avoidable rebounds

When people understand the next step, they are less likely to default back to A&E, 999, or the ward phone. The goal is to make the right support easier to reach than staying confused.

2. A community extension that actually acts

A control centre can see the pressure. This is a mechanism that helps the community layer respond, without asking people to become expert navigators.

3. Cleaner handover language

‚ÄúDischarge advice‚Äù often reads like policy. A Support Playlist translates it into action, in everyday words, with routes and options.

4. Better equity under pressure

The people most at risk are often least able to navigate complexity. This is designed to reduce reliance on insider knowledge.
